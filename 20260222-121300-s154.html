Research Summary¶

This research investigates asymptotically optimal sequential hypothesis testing for data generated by ergodic Markov chains. The focus is on one-sided and α-correct sequential tests, establishing tight non-asymptotic instance-dependent lower bounds on expected stopping times under the alternative hypothesis. The analysis represents a significant advancement over existing bounds, which were either non-asymptotic but instance-independent, or asymptotic but instance-dependent.

Detailed Analysis¶

Theoretical Framework¶

The research addresses sequential hypothesis testing for ergodic Markov chains where:

  • Null Hypothesis (H₀): Unknown transition matrix belongs to prescribed set 𝒫 of stochastic matrices
  • Alternative Hypothesis (H₁): Unknown transition matrix belongs to disjoint set 𝒬
  • Data Generation: Sequential observations from an ergodic Markov chain
  • Test Properties: One-sided and α-correct (controls Type I error at level α)

Key Technical Contributions¶

1. Non-Asymptotic Instance-Dependent Lower Bound¶

The fundamental breakthrough is establishing a lower bound that is: - Non-asymptotic: Valid for finite sample sizes - Instance-dependent: Incorporates specific Markov chain parameters - Tight: Cannot be improved upon in general

This represents a significant improvement over previous work, where bounds were either: - Non-asymptotic but instance-independent (too conservative for specific chains) - Asymptotic but instance-dependent (not valid for finite samples)

2. Incorporation of Markov Chain Structure¶

The lower bound uniquely incorporates: - Stationary distribution (π): Long-term state probabilities - Transition structure: Specific transition probabilities - Spectral properties: Eigenvalues and mixing time characteristics

This comprehensive incorporation allows the bound to adapt to the specific difficulty of the testing problem for different Markov chains.

3. Optimal Test Construction¶

The research proposes an optimal sequential test that: - Achieves the lower bound asymptotically as α→0 - Utilizes a mixture martingale approach - Adapts to unknown transition parameters - Maintains computational tractability

Methodological Innovation¶

Wald's Lemma Extension for Markovian Data¶

The analysis extends classical Wald's lemma to Markovian dependence through: - Poisson equation solutions: Bounding solutions via spectral properties - Concentration analysis: Adapting concentration inequalities to dependent data - Mixture martingales: Constructing appropriate test statistics for dependent sequences

Spectral Property Integration¶

Key to the analysis is the integration of spectral properties: - Second largest eigenvalue modulus (SLEM): Controls mixing rate - Spectral gap: Influences convergence to stationarity - Eigenvalue decomposition: Enables decomposition of transition structure

Mathematical Formulation¶

For an ergodic Markov chain with state space 𝒮 and transition matrix P: - Null hypothesis: P ∈ 𝒫 (prescribed set) - Alternative: P ∈ 𝒬 (disjoint from 𝒫) - Sequential test: Stopping time τ with decision rule δτ - α-correct: Pr(τ < ∞, δτ rejects H₀ | H₀) ≤ α

Lower Bound Structure:

E_P[τ] ≥ (1 + o(1)) × (log(1/α) / D(P || 𝒫)) × f(π, P)

where D(P || 𝒫) is the Kullback-Leibler divergence and f(π, P) incorporates stationary and transition structure.
