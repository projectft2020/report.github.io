## Detailed Analysis

### Theoretical Foundations

#### Online Conformal Prediction
Conformal prediction provides a framework for quantifying prediction uncertainty with valid coverage guarantees. In the online setting, this becomes particularly challenging due to non-stationary data distributions. The work by Liang et al. (2026) extends traditional conformal prediction to handle:

- **Non-stationary environments**: Data streams with unknown distribution drift
- **Online learning**: Sequential decision-making with evolving contexts
- **Training-conditional regret**: Performance evaluation conditioned on training data

#### Regret Framework
The training-conditional regret framework differs from traditional regret measures by:
- Conditioning on the training data used to fit the model
- Focusing on cumulative performance rather than time-averaged metrics
- Providing instance-specific guarantees rather than worst-case bounds

#### Distribution Shift Handling
The research addresses two main types of distribution shift:
1. **Abrupt change points**: Sudden shifts in data distribution
2. **Smooth drift**: Gradual evolution of data characteristics

### Algorithmic Approaches

#### Split-Conformal Algorithm
For scenarios where non-conformity score functions are pretrained on independent datasets:

- **Adaptive calibration sets**: Uses drift detection to dynamically update calibration sets
- **Pretrained models**: Leverages existing models trained on independent data
- **Efficiency**: Computationally efficient for large-scale applications

**Key Innovation**: The incorporation of drift detection mechanisms allows the algorithm to adapt to changing distributions while maintaining statistical guarantees.

#### Full-Conformal Algorithm
For scenarios where non-conformity scores are trained online:

- **Stability-based approach**: Relies on algorithmic stability rather than permutation symmetry
- **Online learning**: Suitable for evolving environments where models must adapt continuously
- **Theoretical guarantees**: Maintains coverage properties under non-stationarity

**Key Innovation**: The use of stability properties makes this approach better suited for online learning under evolving environments compared to traditional conformal methods.
